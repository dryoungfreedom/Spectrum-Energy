# Spectrum Energy Research Framework

**A comprehensive classification system for how materials interact with every energy band.**

The nuclear industry converts fission energy to electricity by boiling water — collapsing gamma rays, X-rays, neutrons, and thermal energy into a single steam cycle. The Spectrum Energy Framework maps what each energy band does to each material, enabling direct conversion pathways for every band.

🌐 **Live Site:** [spectrumenergy.one](https://spectrumenergy.one)

| `GAMMA-GAP-ROADMAP.md` | Research priorities for gamma control |

## The Gamma Problem

At visible wavelengths, we have 100% control — every functional role is filled. At gamma wavelengths, we have 56% control. Four roles are missing:

- **Conductor** — guide gamma along a path
- **Reflector** — bounce gamma directionally  
- **Refractor** — bend gamma through a medium
- **Polarizer** — filter gamma by orientation

Filling these gaps enables the **Spectrum Energy Cell** — a decay battery with no moving parts that harvests every energy band from radioisotope decay.

## Using with Claude AI

### Quick Start

1. **Download the framework**
   - Click the green **Code** button → **Download ZIP**
   - Extract to a folder on your computer

2. **Create a Claude Project**
   - Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai) (requires free account)
   - Click **Projects** in the sidebar
   - Click **Create Project**
   - Name it "Spectrum Energy Research"

3. **Upload the framework files**
   - In your project, click **Add Content** → **Upload Files**
   - Upload these key files:
     - `spectrum-data.js` — the complete materials database
     - `GLOSSARY.md` — terminology reference
     - `GAMMA-GAP-ROADMAP.md` — research priorities

4. **Start researching**
   - Open a new chat in your project
   - Claude now has full context of the framework

### Example Questions

- "What functional roles are missing for gamma control?"
- "Which compounds can transform gamma to x-ray?"
- "What scintillators have the highest density?"
- "Compare thermoelectric materials for the SE Cell"
- "What materials could fill the gamma reflector gap?"
